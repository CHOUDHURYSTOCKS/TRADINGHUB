Option Trading in the Indian Market

Learn the fundamentals of derivatives and options trading

1. Introduction to Derivatives

Derivatives are financial instruments whose value depends on an underlying asset such as stocks, indices, commodities, or currencies. Options are widely traded derivatives in India.

2. What are Options?

An option is a contract that gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an asset at a fixed price within a specific time.

  • Call Option (CE) → Right to BUY
  • Put Option (PE) → Right to SELL

3. Key Terminologies

  • Underlying Asset – e.g., NIFTY, BANK NIFTY, Reliance
  • Strike Price – Predefined price
  • Expiry Date – Last valid day
  • Premium – Cost of option
  • Lot Size – Minimum quantity
  • ITM / ATM / OTM – Moneyness

4. Option Buyers vs Sellers

Buyer

  • Pays premium
  • Limited loss
  • Unlimited profit

Seller

  • Receives premium
  • Limited profit
  • High / unlimited risk

5. Example

NIFTY = 20,000

Buy 20,100 CE at ₹100

  • If NIFTY → 20,300 → Profit ₹100
  • If NIFTY < 20,100 → Loss ₹100

6. Types of Options in India

  • Index Options – NIFTY, BANK NIFTY, FINNIFTY
  • Stock Options – Reliance, TCS, Infosys

Expiry:

  • Weekly (Index)
  • Monthly (Stocks)

7. Option Pricing

Premium = Intrinsic Value + Time Value

  • Market price
  • Volatility
  • Time to expiry
  • Interest rates

8. Greeks

  • Delta – Price sensitivity
  • Theta – Time decay
  • Gamma – Delta change
  • Vega – Volatility impact

9. Basic Strategies

  • Long Call (Bullish)
  • Long Put (Bearish)
  • Covered Call
  • Protective Put
  • Straddle
  • Strangle

10. Advantages

  • High leverage
  • Hedging tool
  • Strategy flexibility
  • Limited loss (buyers)

11. Risks

  • Time decay losses
  • Volatility shocks
  • Complexity
  • Unlimited risk (sellers)

12. Margin in India

  • Buyers → Pay premium only
  • Sellers → SPAN + Exposure margin

13. Regulation

  • SEBI
  • NSE & BSE

14. Beginner Tips

  • Start with index options
  • Focus on buying first
  • Use stop-loss
  • Avoid overtrading
  • Learn risk management

15. Common Mistakes

  • No strategy
  • Ignoring Greeks
  • Overleveraging
  • Emotional trading

16. Conclusion

Options trading offers powerful opportunities but requires discipline, learning, and proper risk management.

17. Practice Plan

  • Week 1–2 → Learn basics
  • Week 3–4 → Paper trading
  • Month 2 → Small capital trading
  • Month 3+ → Advanced strategies
