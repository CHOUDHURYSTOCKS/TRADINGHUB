Step-by-Step Option Chain Analysis

Step 1: Understand the Option Chain

Look at Strike Prices, Call/Put Prices, Open Interest (OI), Volume, and Implied Volatility (IV).

Step 2: Identify Key Levels

Determine ATM, ITM, and OTM strikes to locate potential trading zones.

Step 3: Analyze Open Interest

High Call OI = resistance, High Put OI = support. Helps identify key price levels.

Step 4: Analyze Volume

Compare volume vs OI to determine momentum. Volume >> OI signals new positions forming.

Step 5: Call/Put Ratio (PCR)

PCR = Total Put OI / Total Call OI. Interpretation: PCR < 1 = bullish, PCR > 1 = bearish.

Step 6: Check Premiums & Implied Volatility

Higher premiums & IV indicate expected volatility and potential market fear or optimism.

Step 7: Identify Support & Resistance from OI

Combine high OI strikes with technical charts to confirm key support/resistance levels.

Step 8: Plan Trades

Directional trades, hedges, or spreads can be planned using OI, IV, and PCR data.

Step 9: Monitor Intraday Changes

Track OI, volume, and premiums for spikes that indicate institutional activity or momentum shifts.
