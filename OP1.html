What is Option Trading?

Option trading is a financial derivative strategy where traders buy or sell contracts that give them the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an underlying asset at a predetermined price before a specific date.

Types of Options

Call Option

Gives the right to buy an asset at a fixed price.

Put Option

Gives the right to sell an asset at a fixed price.

Key Terms

Strike Price: Fixed price of the option
Premium: Cost of buying the option
Expiry Date: Last date of validity
Underlying Asset: Stock, index, commodity etc.

How Option Trading Works

Traders predict market direction:
  • If price rises → Call option gains value
  • If price falls → Put option gains value
Profit depends on movement, volatility, and time decay.

Option Strategies

Buying Calls: Bullish strategy
Buying Puts: Bearish strategy
Covered Call: Income strategy using stock holding
Straddle: Profit from volatility
Strangle: Wider volatility strategy
Iron Condor: Range-bound market strategy

Greeks in Options

Delta: Price sensitivity to underlying asset
Gamma: Rate of change of Delta
Theta: Time decay effect
Vega: Volatility impact

Advantages

  • High profit potential with low capital
  • Hedging against risk
  • Flexible strategies

Risks

  • Time decay can reduce value quickly
  • High volatility risk
  • Complex for beginners
  • Possibility of total premium loss

Beginner Tips

  • Start with paper trading
  • Understand Greeks before trading
  • Avoid over-leveraging
  • Focus on risk management
