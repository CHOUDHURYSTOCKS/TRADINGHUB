OPTION Analysis for Day Trading

Core Option Chain Terms (Simplified for Trading)

1. Max Pain (Option Pain Theory)

• Definition: The strike price where option buyers lose the most money and option sellers gain the most.

• Calculated based on total open interest (OI) of calls & puts.

👉 Trading Insight:
Price often moves toward Max Pain near expiry (especially weekly expiry for Nifty). Acts like a magnet level.
👉 Use:
If market is far from Max Pain → expect pull toward it. Near expiry → good for range-bound strategies.

2. OI (Open Interest)

• Definition: Total number of outstanding option contracts (not yet squared off).

👉 Interpretation: High OI at a strike = strong support/resistance
👉 Use: Highest Call OI → Resistance; Highest Put OI → Support

3. Change in OI (ΔOI)

• Definition: Increase or decrease in OI during the session.

Very important for intraday traders.
Price Move OI Change Meaning
↑ Price ↑ OI Long buildup → Bullish
↓ Price ↑ OI Short buildup → Bearish
↑ Price ↓ OI Short covering → Bullish reversal
↓ Price ↓ OI Long unwinding → Bearish reversal
👉 Use: Helps identify who is in control (buyers/sellers)

4. PCR (Put Call Ratio)

• Definition: Ratio of total Put OI to Call OI.

PCR Value Meaning
> 1 More puts → Bullish sentiment
< 1 More calls → Bearish sentiment
~1 Neutral
👉 Advanced View: Very high PCR (>1.5) → Over-bullish → possible reversal; Very low PCR (<0.7) → Over-bearish → bounce possible

5. Strike Price Importance

• ATM (At The Money), ITM (In The Money), OTM (Out of The Money)

👉 Use: ATM strikes → highest activity; OTM writing → common for intraday option sellers

6. Call Writing vs Put Writing

📉 Call Writing: Bearish / resistance formation, When call OI increases

📈 Put Writing: Bullish / support formation, When put OI increases

👉 Golden Rule: Follow where writers are building positions, not buyers

7. OI Build-up Zones (Support & Resistance)

👉 Example: 22,000 CE high OI → resistance, 21,800 PE high OI → support. Market tends to move within these zones unless breakout occurs.

8. Unwinding

• When OI decreases

👉 Types: Call unwinding → bullish, Put unwinding → bearish

9. Short Covering

• When traders who sold earlier exit positions

👉 Signal: Price ↑ + OI ↓ → fast upward move possible

10. Long Liquidation

• Price ↓ + OI ↓

👉 Signal: Weakness, but not strong bearish trend

Practical Daily Trading Strategy (Simple Framework)

Step 1: Identify Range

• Highest Call OI = Resistance

• Highest Put OI = Support

Trade inside range unless breakout

Step 2: Track Change in OI

• Where is fresh writing happening? That level becomes strong S/R

Step 3: Watch PCR

• Extreme PCR → possible reversal

Step 4: Check Max Pain (especially expiry day)

• Expect price gravitation toward it

Step 5: Combine with Price Action

• Option chain alone is NOT enough. Confirm with:

  • Candlestick pattern
  • VWAP
  • Trend direction

Important Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Blindly trading based only on PCR
❌ Ignoring change in OI
❌ Trading against strong writers
❌ Not considering expiry effects

Pro Tips (Used by Experienced Traders)

• Weekly expiry (Thursday) → Max Pain works best
• Morning OI setup often traps traders → wait 30–60 mins
• Sudden OI spikes → institutional activity
• Combine with Bank Nifty for confirmation

Extra Concepts Worth Following

  • IV (Implied Volatility)
  • VWAP
  • Delta / Gamma (advanced)
  • Volume + OI combination
  • Option Greeks (for deeper edge)
