NIFTY 50 Intraday Option Metrics

1. PCR (Put Call Ratio)

What it is: Ratio of total Put Open Interest (OI) to Call Open Interest.

PCR = Put OI / Call OI

Interpretation (Intraday):

  • PCR > 1 → More puts than calls → Bullish bias
  • PCR < 1 → More calls than puts → Bearish bias

Day Trading Insight:

  • Very high PCR (>1.5) → Market may be overly bullish → possible reversal down
  • Very low PCR (<0.7) → Market may be overly bearish → possible bounce
  • Watch PCR trend, not just value:
    • Rising PCR + rising price = strong bullish confirmation
    • Falling PCR + falling price = bearish continuation
2. Max Pain

What it is: Strike price where maximum option sellers (writers) profit and most buyers lose.

Interpretation:

  • Market often gravitates toward Max Pain near expiry (especially weekly expiry in NIFTY)
  • Acts like a magnet price

Day Trading Use:

  • Price far from Max Pain → potential pull toward it
  • Price near Max Pain → expect range-bound movement
  • Combine with OI data:
    • Strong call OI above → resistance
    • Strong put OI below → support
3. ATM IV (At-The-Money Implied Volatility)

What it is: Implied volatility of the strike closest to current NIFTY price.

Interpretation:

  • High ATM IV → Options expensive, big move expected, good for option sellers
  • Low ATM IV → Options cheap, small move expected, good for option buyers

Intraday Use:

  • Sudden IV spike → often before/after big move
  • IV crush → option premiums drop even if price moves slightly
4. IV Percentile (IVP)

What it is: Shows where current IV stands compared to its historical range (usually 1 year). Scale: 0 to 100

Interpretation:

  • IVP > 80 → IV is very high vs history → favor selling strategies
  • IVP < 20 → IV is very low → favor buying strategies
  • IVP ~50 → Neutral → no strong volatility edge

Intraday Angle:

  • High IVP → sell straddle/strangle (if experienced)
  • Low IVP → buy options before breakout

Practical Intraday Setup

Bullish Setup Example:

  • PCR rising above 1
  • Price holding above strong Put OI support
  • Max Pain below current price (room to move up)
  • ATM IV moderate (not too high)
  • → Consider buying CE or bull spread

Bearish Setup Example:

  • PCR falling below 1
  • Strong Call OI resistance above
  • Price below Max Pain
  • IV rising (fear building)
  • → Consider buying PE or bear spread

Range-Bound Setup:

  • Price near Max Pain
  • PCR stable around 1
  • High IVP
  • → Favor option selling (iron fly / straddle)

Simple Memory Shortcut:

  • PCR → Direction bias
  • Max Pain → Magnet level
  • ATM IV → Cost of options
  • IV Percentile → Strategy choice (buy vs sell)
